Study Materials Standard

Research-backed session schema and content guidelines for building certification exam prep courses. All sessions follow src/data/*.js data files and the SessionCourse.jsx renderer.

Why this standard exists: Research from Dunlosky et al. (2013) ranked study techniques by effectiveness. Only practice testing and distributed practice rated HIGH. This standard bakes in high-utility techniques so every session is effective by default, preventing low-value study methods.
Standard

Overview

What makes a study session research-backed and how it maps to the exam prep cycle.

The Research Foundation

TechniqueResearch ratingImplementation in sessions
Practice testing / retrievalHIGHpreLearningCheck + microQuizzes + sample question
Spaced / distributed practiceHIGHCheckpoint state persisted to Supabase; cross-device sync
Elaborative interrogationModerate"Why?" prompts after SpeedBump quiz correct answers
Self-explanationModeratePrompt shown before the sample question
InterleavingModerateMix topics from previous sessions in later practice

Session Lifecycle

  1. Pre-test (preLearningCheck) — One question on the session's core concept. Shown before any teaching content. Research shows attempting retrieval before studying improves retention even on wrong answers.
  2. Teaching sections — Narrative + structured content (bullets, tables, callouts). Sections 0–1 are pure prose; sections 2+ mix narrative leads with structured detail.
  3. Retrieval checkpoints (microQuizzes) — 1 quiz per 2–3 sections. Scenario-based multiple choice. Learner must pass to unlock next section. State persisted to Supabase.
  4. Self-explanation prompt — Shown before the final practice question. Learner reflects on the core concept without seeing an answer yet.
  5. Sample exam question — Realistic business/scenario stem (never definition recall). Includes per-option explanation of why each choice is right or wrong.
  6. Video + reference material — Supplementary YouTube video + key terms + AWS services + exam tips. Shown after the sample question.

Guidelines

Writing Guidelines

How to author each section type for clarity, retention, and exam readiness.

Section Narrative Arc

  • Sections 0–1 (pure narrative): Real-world story first, then the problem it illustrates. Goal: make learners feel the need for the concept before defining it. No bullets, no tables.
  • Section 2 (bridge): One short narrative paragraph, then transition to first structured content (usually a table or bullets).
  • Sections 3+ (fully structured): Lead with one sentence, then bullets/table/callout. Every section is self-contained and scannable.

Content Principles

  • Conversational tone throughout. Write for someone smart but new to the domain. Avoid jargon until it's defined.
  • Every table = a decision the exam will force. Tables should answer "when to use X vs Y" — not just list facts.
  • Every tip callout = one rule that resolves a class of exam questions. One sentence.
  • Every warning callout = the most common distractor and why students fall for it.
  • Paragraph breaks matter. Split long prose with \n\n into 2–3 focused paragraphs. No walls of text.

preLearningCheck

  • Tests the session's primary concept — not a detail, not trivia.
  • All four options must be plausible before reading.
  • note must normalize failure: "Attempting this before reading improves retention even on wrong answers."

microQuizzes

  • Minimum: 1 per 2 sections. Ideal: 1 per 2–3 sections.
  • Question must be scenario-based — not "what is the definition of X?"
  • elaborativePrompt asks why or how, never what. It has no answer in the UI — it triggers reflection.
  • Good elaborativePrompt: "Why would using a supervised model here require more setup than unsupervised?"
  • Bad elaborativePrompt: "What is supervised learning?"

selfExplanationPrompt

  • One or two sentences placed before the sample question.
  • Frame as: "Before trying the question, explain to yourself: [core contrast or concept]."
  • Do not hint at the sample question's answer.

examTips

  • Format: "[signal in question stem] → [answer]"
  • Each tip neutralizes a specific distractor.
  • 3–5 tips; quality over quantity.

Sample Question

  • Stem: Always a business or engineering scenario. A company / developer / team wants X. Which approach fits best?
  • Never: "Which of the following is the definition of…?"
  • perOption: Every wrong option needs an explanation of why it's wrong. This is where the deepest exam prep happens.
QA

Content Checklist

Verify before committing any session.

Structure

  • ☐ preLearningCheck tests the session's single most important concept
  • ☐ At least 1 microQuiz per 2 teaching sections
  • ☐ Every microQuiz has an elaborativePrompt asking WHY or HOW
  • ☐ Sections 0–1 are pure narrative (no bullets/table)
  • ☐ Long body strings use \n\n for paragraph breaks — no single wall of text
  • ☐ selfExplanationPrompt is present and doesn't hint at the sample answer

Content Quality

  • ☐ videos has at least one relevant public YouTube video
  • ☐ Every AWS service mentioned in sections appears in awsServices[]
  • ☐ sample stem is scenario-based, not definition-recall
  • ☐ sample.explanation.perOption explains WHY each wrong answer is wrong
  • ☐ examTips follow the "[signal] → [answer]" format

Common Mistakes to Avoid

MistakeWhy it failsFix
All sections start with bulletsLearners skip to bullets without contextSections 0–1 must be pure prose with a real-world hook
preLearningCheck tests a detailDoesn't prime the most important memory traceTest the ONE thing you most want them to remember
microQuiz is definition recallLow cognitive depth; won't transfer to examRewrite as scenario: "A company needs to… which approach?"
elaborativePrompt has an obvious answerLearner skips the reflectionMake it genuinely open: "Why does X exist at all, given Y?"
Single wall-of-text body paragraphHard to read; no visual breathing roomSplit with \n\n into 2–3 short focused paragraphs
Sample question stem is a definitionNot what the real exam looks likeAlways use a business/engineering scenario
examTips list facts instead of decision rulesLearner still has to figure out when to apply themFormat as "[when you see this signal] → [do this]"
Reference

Real Examples

Live sessions that follow this standard. Use these as templates.

AIF-C01 Course (src/data/aifC01Course.js)

All 16 sessions follow the standard. Key examples:

  • Session 1: d1-s1 — Covers AI/ML/Deep Learning hierarchy. Opens with Netflix & spam filters (real-world story), builds to formal definitions.
  • Session 3: d1-s3 — ML pipeline & metrics. Opens with the story of a rejected loan applicant asking "why?" (connects to interpretability later in Domain 4).
  • Session 4: d2-s4 — Generative AI fundamentals. Starts with "the most powerful autocomplete ever built" → tokens/embeddings/transformers.
  • Session 8: d3-s8 — RAG. Opens with "the open-book exam" analogy: two students, same knowledge, but one has access to relevant sources at question time.

Why This Pattern Works for Exams

  • Spaced retrieval: Pre-test → teaching → microQuizzes → sample question. Multiple retrieval cycles, spaced over a single session.
  • Transfer to exam format: Sample questions use the exact same scenario-based stem format as real exams. Exam tips encode decision rules learners can apply in 80 seconds.
  • Reducing cognitive load: Narrative first (low barrier to entry) → structured content (scannable). Learners get the context before they see the reference material.
  • Reducing hallucination: Elaborative prompts force learners to *generate* explanations (high-utility technique), not just read ours. This deepens encoding and surfaces gaps in understanding.

Implementation Files

Where the standard lives in the codebase.

Source

  • ~/.claude/skills/create-study-session.md — The authoritative skill file with full schema + guidelines. Check here for edge cases.

Data

  • src/data/aifC01Course.js — AWS Certified AI Practitioner course. 16 sessions, all following the standard.
  • src/data/*.js — Other certification courses (DVA, Azure, GCP). Same schema.

Components

  • src/components/study/SessionCourse.jsx — Renders the standard session structure. Handles navigation, collapsible sections, microQuiz state, video embeds.
  • src/components/study/PreLearningCheck.jsx — Pre-test component. Prevents cheating with state reset between attempts.
  • src/components/study/SpeedBump.jsx — microQuiz component. Locks next section until passed. Persists state to Supabase.
  • src/components/study/ExamQuestion.jsx — Sample question + per-option explanations.
